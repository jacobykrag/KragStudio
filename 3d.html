My 3D Workflow

Step by step:

1. Modeling the Asset

My 3D workflow is a step-by-step process that ensures clean models, efficient UVs, and high-quality textures. It starts with modeling the asset in Blender, where I focus on creating clean, optimized geometry that balances detail with performance. Whether it’s hard surface or organic modeling, I make sure the topology is well-structured for unwrapping and texturing later.

2. Marking Seams

Once the modeling is complete, I move on to marking seams on the model. This step is crucial for defining how the mesh will be cut into UV islands, ensuring that the unwrapped UVs are both logical and efficient.

3. UV Unwrapping

After the seams are set, I unwrap the UVs, paying close attention to making the best use of the available texture space while maintaining consistent texel density across the entire model.

4. Applying a Checker Map and Baking a Material ID Map

To check for any potential issues, I apply a checker map to the UVs. This visual guide helps me spot any areas where stretching or distortion might occur, and I make adjustments to the UV layout as needed to eliminate those problems. Once the UVs are clean and ready, I bake a Material ID map in Blender. This map assigns different colors to various parts of the model, making it much easier to isolate and apply materials later in the texturing process.

5. Texturing in Substance Painter

The next step is bringing the model into Substance Painter, where I set up my texturing workflow. I import the model and the baked maps, organizing them in a way that allows me to efficiently build detailed, high-quality textures. Substance Painter is where I bring the model to life, adding materials, details, and effects to make it look polished and realistic.

6. Exporting and Integration

Once the texturing is complete, I export all the necessary texture maps—like albedo, roughness, metallic, and normal maps—ensuring they are optimized for the target platform or engine. From there, I integrate the model into a game engine like Unreal or Unity or prepare it for rendering in another application.

7. Final Thoughts

This methodical process allows me to create visually appealing and technically sound assets, ready for use in any 3D pipeline.
